Making ethical decisions

While working at a hostel, a guest failed to show up for their reservation due to a car accident. The reservation was non-refundable, and according to the hostel's policy, the full amount was to be charged for no-shows.

In this scenario, the values of empathy, compassion, and flexibility were crucial. It's essential to recognize the human aspect of the situation and the unexpected challenges the guest faced

Despite not having immediate approval from a manager, you decided to waive the cancellation fee for the guest. Your decision stemmed from your belief in being flexible and understanding in extraordinary circumstances, even if it meant going against company policies.

Looking back on the decision, you realize that it was aligned with your personal values of empathy and compassion. While it may have deviated from the company policy, you believe it was the right thing to do considering the circumstances. The experience reinforced the importance of balancing company guidelines with human empathy, especially in situations where guests encounter unforeseen difficulties.

My background and its influence in my identity

Growing up in Mexico has profoundly shaped my values and identity. The vibrant culture, rich traditions, and close-knit community have all played significant roles in shaping who I am today.

Mexican culture places a strong emphasis on family and community. From a young age, I was taught the importance of family bonds, respect for others, and the value of spending quality time together. My culture is known for its warmth, hospitality, and emphasis on relationships. Whether it's sharing a meal with loved ones, celebrating festivals and holidays, or participating in community gatherings, these experiences have taught me the importance of building meaningful connections and supporting one another through both joyous and challenging times.

The people around me, including family members, friends, and mentors, have also played a pivotal role in shaping my values and identity. Through their guidance, wisdom, and encouragement, I have learned the importance of hard work, perseverance, and resilience. They have instilled in me a sense of pride in my heritage and a desire to contribute positively to my community and society at large.

Additionally, I have been able to live in other countries in the world where I have been influenced by other cultures and customs. This multicultural environment has fostered a deep appreciation for diversity and inclusion, encouraging me to embrace different viewpoints and celebrate the richness of our collective heritage.

Strengths and my learning journey

My strengths of honesty, commitment, and perseverance will be invaluable assets during my learning journey. This may limit opportunities for collaboration and professional advancement, particularly in environments that prioritize extroverted qualities.

Working productively with others and its difficulties

While working at a hostel, I was part of a team responsible for managing guest services and ensuring a pleasant stay for visitors. One busy weekend, we encountered tension among staff members regarding the distribution of workload during busy hours.

To address the tension, I made team meetings to discuss workload distribution and find solutions together. We brainstormed ideas to streamline tasks and redistribute responsibilities based on each team member's strengths and availability. Also, I encouraged open communication for the team members to express their concerns and suggest improvements.

While the team meetings and open communication channels helped to some extent, there were still instances of resistance and frustration among staff members. Some team members felt overwhelmed with their workload, while others believed that certain tasks were unfairly distributed.

Reflecting on the situation, I realized that a more structured approach to task delegation and regular check-ins could have been beneficial. In the future, I would implement a rotating schedule to ensure fair distribution of tasks among team members and reduce the likelihood of burnout. I would also conduct regular performance reviews to identify areas for improvement and provide support and training where needed.
